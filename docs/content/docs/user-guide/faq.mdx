# FAQ and Troubleshooting

This page covers the most common user questions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### I am a student and cannot see the submit button

Check the following:

- Have you launched the workspace for this assignment yet?
- Are you looking at the correct assignment?
- Has the due date already passed?

### I submitted my work. Why do I not see my grade?

Most likely, your teacher has not published grades yet.

Leopard can store a grade before making it visible to students.

### I am a teacher and do not see teacher tools

Your account may not have teacher access yet.

Contact the person who manages Leopard for your organization or course.

### A workspace takes a while to load. Is that normal?

Yes. Leopard may need time to prepare the coding environment. Waiting around 30 seconds is normal.

### What if I submitted the wrong version?

In the current Leopard student flow, submission is treated as final once you confirm it.

If you submitted the wrong version, contact your teacher.

### Do students see grades as soon as I save them?

No. Students see grades only after you publish them.

## Troubleshooting for Students

If something is wrong, try these checks first:

- Refresh the page.
- Make sure you are signed into the correct account.
- Re-open the classroom from the dashboard.
- Re-open the assignment and check the status panel.
- Confirm whether the due date has passed.
- Ask your teacher whether grades have been published.

## Troubleshooting for Teachers

If something is wrong, try these checks first:

- Refresh the classroom or assignment page.
- Confirm that students are enrolled in the correct classroom.
- Open the assignment directly from the classroom page.
- Check whether a submission exists before trying to review it.
- Save grade and feedback first, then publish grades when review is complete.

## The Short Version

If you want the shortest explanation of Leopard:

- Students join classes, launch workspaces, complete assignments, and submit before the deadline.
- Teachers create classes, post assignments, review work with replay, grade submissions, and publish grades.
